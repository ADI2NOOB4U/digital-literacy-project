Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details by pretending to be a trusted source. 

It usually relies on creating a false sense of urgency or trust so that the user acts quickly without verifying the authenticity.


A common example can be seen with students. Imagine a student receiving an email that claims to offer a paid internship opportunity. 


The email looks professional, uses formal language, and even includes a company logo, making it appear genuine. 


It asks the student to click on a link and fill out a form to apply quickly, creating pressure to act fast.


Without properly checking the sender’s email address or verifying the link, the student clicks on it and submits personal details, including login credentials.


At that moment, the student believes they are applying for a legitimate opportunity.


However, after some time, the student starts noticing suspicious activities such as unauthorized login attempts, password reset notifications, or unusual account behavior.


This happens because the attacker has gained access to the student’s information and is misusing it.


Phishing attacks often target students and beginners because they may not be fully aware of such scams or may overlook small warning signs like suspicious links or unknown email addresses.


The consequences can be serious, including data theft, financial loss, and misuse of personal information.



This case clearly highlights the importance of being aware and cautious while interacting online. 


It shows that users should always verify the source, avoid clicking on unknown links, and think carefully before sharing any sensitive information.
